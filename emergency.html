Emergency Procedure

Many situations can lead to an emergency procedure. Use the quick links below to navigate to each scenario:

High RPV Pressure High RPV Temperature High Water Level Low Water Level Very Low Water Level Fuel Channel Leak Loss of Offsite Power (LOOP)

High RPV Pressure

Description: The pressure inside the Reactor Pressure Vessel (RPV) is above safe limits. This may be due to steam buildup or low steam output. High pressure can damage the reactor.

  • Open relief valves to release pressure.
  • Insert control rods to reduce reactor power if needed.
  • Increase turbine/bypass steam flow to lower pressure in the reactor.
  • Monitor RPV pressure instruments.
  • Press AZ-5 if pressure remains too high or continues to rise.

High RPV Temperature

Description: The temperature inside the RPV is higher than safety limits. This may result from inadequate coolant flow and can cause fuel damage.

  • Verify circulation pumps are running.
  • Insert control rods to lower temperature if recirc pumps are not enough.
  • Monitor temperature gauges constantly.
  • Initiate SCRAM if a critical temperature is reached.

High Water Level

Description: Excessive water level in the primary coolant circuit. Can cause water hammer or flooding. Often due to feedwater over-injection.

  • Reduce feedwater flow.
  • Open recirculation drain valves to lower water level.
  • Monitor water level indicators.

Low Water Level

Description: Water level in the core is below normal. This reduces cooling and increases fuel overheating risk.

  • Do not increase reactor power.
  • Increase feedwater injection.
  • Monitor water levels closely.
  • Be ready to activate ECCS.
  • Press AZ-5 if levels continue to drop.

Very Low Water Level

Description: Critical low water level alarm. Core may be exposed. Immediate action required.

  • Press AZ-5 immediately.
  • Activate ECCS to raise water level.
  • Ensure feedwater pumps are running.
  • Monitor RPV temperature and pressure.

Fuel Channel Leak

Description: Fuel channel ruptured. May cause power surges and water level drop beyond feedwater recovery capability.

  • Activate all recirculation pumps to full speed.
  • Open all relief valves.
  • Do NOT SCRAM - use shutdown rod controls.
  • Start OCC pump (Offline Core Cooling).
  • Monitor all core parameters.
  • Call engineer if shutdown is successful.
    • Note: 80% chance of catastrophic failure. Good luck.

Loss of Offsite Power (LOOP)

Description: Offsite power grid has failed. All systems run on onsite diesel generators. Core cooling is critical.

  • Start 1 EDG (Emergency Diesel Generator).
  • Ensure core cooling systems and ECCS are operational.
  • Monitor temperature, pressure, and water level.
  • Initiate ECCS if water level is low.
  • SCRAM the reactor if unstable.
  • Wait for return of offsite power.
